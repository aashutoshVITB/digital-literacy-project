# Cybercrime Awareness Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It involves scammers tricking people into authorizing fraudulent transactions through UPI apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. These scams often rely on social engineering techniques where the victim is manipulated into sharing sensitive information or approving payment requests.

## How the Crime Typically Happens
A typical UPI fraud begins when a scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media message. The scammer may pretend to be a bank representative, customer support agent, or even a buyer from an online marketplace.  

First, the scammer gains the victim’s trust by claiming there is an issue with their bank account or by offering to send money for a purchase. Next, the victim is asked to click a link or approve a "collect request" in their UPI app. In some cases, the victim may also be asked to share an OTP (One-Time Password).  

Once the victim approves the request or shares the OTP, money is transferred directly from their bank account to the scammer’s account. Because the transaction is authorized by the user, it becomes difficult to reverse.

## Who is Targeted
UPI fraud often targets students, elderly individuals, and people who are not very familiar with digital payment systems.

## Consequences
Victims may lose significant amounts of money and may struggle to recover the funds. These incidents can also lead to stress, financial loss, and loss of trust in online payment systems.
