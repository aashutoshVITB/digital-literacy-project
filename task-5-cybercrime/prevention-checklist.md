# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

As college students increasingly rely on the internet for learning, communication, and financial transactions, it is important to practice safe online behavior. The following checklist provides practical steps to prevent common cybercrimes.

## Prevention Tips

- **Use strong and unique passwords** for your accounts and avoid using the same password for multiple platforms.
- **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on important accounts such as email, social media, and banking apps.
- **Never share OTPs, PINs, or passwords** with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank or a trusted organization.
- **Avoid clicking suspicious links** received through emails, SMS, or social media messages.
- **Always verify the sender** before responding to messages that ask for personal or financial information.
- **UPI Safety Tip:** Never approve a **collect request** in a UPI app if you are not expecting to receive money.
- **UPI Safety Tip:** Do not scan unknown QR codes or accept payment requests from strangers.
- **Keep your phone and apps updated** to ensure you have the latest security protections.
- **Log out of accounts on shared or public computers** to prevent unauthorized access.

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you experience or suspect cyber fraud, report it immediately through the following channels:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- **Cybercrime Helpline:** 1930

Reporting incidents quickly can increase the chances of stopping fraudulent transactions and recovering lost funds.
